2000-Watt Society Calculator

Estimate your continuous primary-energy footprint and see how it compares to the 2000-watt target.

The 2000-Watt Society is an initiative born at ETH Zürich that proposes a long-term goal: every person on Earth living on no more than 2000 watts of continuous primary energy — roughly 17,500 kWh per year, including everything from heating to flights to the food on your plate. For reference, the average Western European is currently at 5000–6000 W, and the average American is closer to 12,000 W.

What is "primary energy"?

Primary energy includes all the energy used along the way — not just the electricity that arrives at your meter, but the energy lost generating and transporting it, the embodied energy in the food and goods you consume, and a share of public infrastructure (roads, hospitals, schools). That last category is a fixed ~600 W per person and is included in this estimate by default.
